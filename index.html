What It Is

Your Playlist.
Pressed in Wax.

BurnVinyl lets you take any songs from your digital library and burn them permanently onto a real vinyl record — the same way music was pressed for decades. Except now, you choose what goes on it.

Each blank record holds 40 minutes of music. Twenty on Side A. Twenty on Side B. Pick your tracks, fill the grooves, and press burn. Once it's done, those songs live in that record forever. You can't undo it. You can't overwrite it. That's the point.

The result is something streaming will never give you — a permanent, physical object that holds the music you chose, on the day you chose it.

The Process

How to Burn
a Record

01

Pick Your Songs

Open the BurnVinyl app and choose tracks from your music library. Fill Side A, then Side B. You have 20 minutes per side — so every song is a decision.

02

Review the Press

Preview the track order, timing, and side splits. Rearrange until it feels right. Once you're satisfied — and only once — you commit to the burn.

03

Burn It

Tap Burn. Your music is cut into the grooves of the record. When it's done, those songs are sealed in — permanently, irrevocably yours.

04

Drop the Needle

Place it on any turntable and play. Your music. Your sequence. Pressed into vinyl. No subscription. No algorithm. No expiry date.

The Details

Built Around
the Ritual

I

One Burn Per Record

Every blank record has exactly one burn in it. Once your songs are in the grooves, they're permanent. There's no second chance — and that's what makes the first one count.

II

40 Minutes of Music

Twenty minutes per side, just like a classic LP. Enough for a tight album, a long drive, or a mixtape built with intention. Every second is a choice you can't take back.

III

Any Song You Own

Burn tracks from your personal library — songs you've bought, ripped, or own outright. The BurnVinyl app makes selecting, sequencing, and previewing your record easy before you commit.

IV

Plays on Any Turntable

A BurnVinyl record is a real vinyl record. It plays on any standard turntable, at 33⅓ RPM, with the warmth and depth the format is known for. No special hardware needed to listen.

V

High Fidelity Sound

The burn process preserves the full depth and warmth of your audio. No compression artifacts, no digital thinness — just your music, cut into the groove the way it deserves to be heard.

VI

Physical Ownership

No subscription. No streaming rights. No one can revoke it. When the record is burned, it's an object — yours, forever, independent of any platform or service that may disappear tomorrow.
